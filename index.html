Publications

LORA users beware: A few spurious tokens can manipulate your finetuned model

Published in arXiv (Preprint), 2025

This work reveals a critical vulnerability in Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) for LLM finetuning: even a single spuriously correlated token can manipulate model predictions. We introduce Seamless Spurious Token Injection (SSTI) and demonstrate that more resource-efficient LoRA setups are paradoxically more vulnerable to attacks. Our experiments across multiple model families and datasets show that existing data sanitization methods fail to prevent these manipulations, raising significant concerns for AI safety and data quality in production systems.

Recommended citation: Marcel Mateos Salles*, Praney Goyal*, Pradyut Sekhsaria, Hai Huang, Randall Balestriero (2025). LORA users beware: A few spurious tokens can manipulate your finetuned model. arXiv preprint arXiv:2506.11402. https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.11402

Paraphrasing Away Malicious Tokens: Improving LLM Finetuning Safety by Filtering Spurious Correlation

Published in NeurIPS 2025 Workshop on Evaluating the Evolving LLM Lifecycle (Poster), 2025

This paper investigates how LoRA-finetuned LLMs are vulnerable to Seamless Spurious Token Injection (SSTI), where a single malicious token can dominate predictions through data poisoning or artifacts. Testing across three model families and four datasets, we evaluate LLM-based paraphrasing as a defense mechanism. Our findings show that minimal injection suffices to manipulate outputs, while paraphrasing offers only partial protection, exposing a fundamental efficiency-robustness tradeoff in parameter-efficient finetuning.

Recommended citation: Marcel Mateos Salles*, Praney Goyal*, Pradyut Sekhsaria, Hai Huang, Randall Balestriero (2025). Paraphrasing Away Malicious Tokens: Improving LLM Finetuning Safety by Filtering Spurious Correlation. NeurIPS 2025 Workshop on Evaluating the Evolving LLM Lifecycle. https://openreview.net/forum?id=7fM4Q0TLgZ
